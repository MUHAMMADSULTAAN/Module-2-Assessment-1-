Polar Bear

The polar bear is a large bear species found in the Arctic. They are known for their thick white fur and layer of fat that keeps them warm in icy conditions.

Habitat

Polar bears primarily inhabit the Arctic ice caps and coastal regions, relying on sea ice to hunt for seals, their primary food source.

Diet

They mainly eat seals, but will also consume other marine mammals and birds if food is scarce.

Interesting Fact

Polar bears have a keen sense of smell and can detect seals nearly a mile away, even under several feet of compacted snow.
